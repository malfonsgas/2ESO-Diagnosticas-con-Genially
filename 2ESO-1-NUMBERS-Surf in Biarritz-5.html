Your instructor has explained that when you surf, there are several forces at work.

The forces of gravity and buoyancy are working against each other. Gravity is pulling the surfboard down. Buoyancy is pushing it up. Which force is greater? It depends on the density of the surfboard’s materials. As you can guess, the materials used in surfboards today are much lighter and less dense than the wooden materials used in the past.

The surfboard you are going to use, is made with foam in the interior, coated with fibre and polyester. It has a density of 30.44 kg/m3.

The volume of your board is a measurement of the total amount of space that your board occupies. Volume helps you to catch waves. It helps you to paddle easier and it helps you to get going when the waves are small, or flat.

The volume of the board you need depends on your weigh. In your case, you need a 55 litres board.
